Dear Shae,

I’d like to start by sharing that your poetry moves me to tears,
and quite honestly, I don’t know if prose has ever touched me so deeply.

a tear rolled down the right
another down the left

whole and holy
unbereft
whole and holy
unbereft
Something remembered
And since forgotten

For me, this feels visceral;

somatic.

It reminds me of flying home to Minneapolis this past Sunday.
There was a sensation I noticed in my chest. It felt uncomfortable and a little overwhelming. Curious about this, I tried to describe it.

“Like sitting by a warm fire… not hot. Just warm”

I laughed when I realized that it didn’t sound uncomfortable at all. But it was like the difference between anxiety and excitement. So I opened to it, and softened around it…

“you are welcome here… what are you trying to say?”

It didn’t need to be named yet, but I chose to soften around it. Something changed. Not the sensation, but the container holding it.

a tear rolled down the left
another down the right

like twin stars
falling in the night
Walking in a memory
Quiet forest
Quick heartbeat
Calling out, no sound
I felt this deeply: a rush of sensation along the back of my neck, then across my shoulders and then into my heartspace. I don’t need to give it meaning, but someplace within me remembers too.
